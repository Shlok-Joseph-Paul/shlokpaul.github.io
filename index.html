About

About me

My research focuses on solution-processed semiconductor nanomaterials and optoelectronic devices, especially colloidal quantum dots for near- and short-wave infrared detection. I develop materials, device architectures, fabrication methods, and characterization workflows that connect fundamental photophysics to practical device performance.

I am especially interested in heavy-metal-free infrared materials, scalable thin-film deposition, device automation, and the use of modern data and machine-learning workflows to accelerate experimental materials discovery.

Current themes

  • Infrared-active colloidal nanomaterials for practical optoelectronics
  • Measurement systems that make device studies faster and more reproducible
  • Data-rich experimental workflows that support scientific scale-up

Research

Selected research directions

Photodetectors

Heavy-metal-free Ag₂Se quantum dot photodiodes

Developed solution-phase ligand exchanged Ag₂Se quantum-dot inks and integrated them into infrared photodiodes with strong detectivity, fast response, and operation in the near-to-short-wave infrared.

Scalable fabrication

Biphasic dip-coating for CQD thin films

Built automated layer-by-layer dip-coating workflows and explored biphasic coating geometries that reduce material waste while enabling uniform large-area and non-planar quantum-dot films.

Devices

Transparent electrodes for top-illuminated devices

Worked on transparent Ag-nanowire/polymer electrodes for infrared photodetectors, including flexible and top-illuminated device architectures.

Current interests

Carrier dynamics, gain, and AI-for-science workflows

Current interests include quantum-dot carrier dynamics, amplified spontaneous emission, transient optical/electrical measurements, high-throughput experimentation, and data-driven materials optimization.

Projects

Technical portfolio

Beyond publications, I build experimental tools and analysis workflows that make device fabrication and characterization more reproducible, scalable, and data-rich.

This work sits between bench science and infrastructure: building the systems that make better experiments possible.

Automated three-stage dip-coater

Motorized adsorption, ligand exchange, and rinsing workflow for layer-by-layer CQD film fabrication.

Photodetector characterization workflows

IV, responsivity, noise, transient response, and frequency-response measurements using lock-in amplifiers, SMUs, and current preamplifiers.

Data analysis and visualization

Python and Origin-based workflows for extracting device metrics, plotting datasets, and preparing publication-quality figures.
